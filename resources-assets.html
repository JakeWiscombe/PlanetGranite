Design Assets

Izzy's post

Before this point it was mostly just group discussion and deciding what we wanted to do for the game.

23/03–29/03: Created animated sprites for the player spaceship with damage animation, two alien enemy variations, two asteroid variations, a fuel bar, a bullet, an energy sparkle (unused in final version of game), and a background artwork (also unused as it did not fit the final theme).

30/03–05/04: Animated a planet with aseprite but realised this would be unsustainable for the amount of planets we needed; found tool on itch.io that allowed me to create planets much faster and in much greater detail than I could do by hand. Created damage animations for the above two enemies, an asteroid explosion animation. Made five animated planets using the pixel planet generator.

06/04–12/04: Made another six animated planets with the program.

13/04–19/04: Made six animated background tiles. Began making a list of possible fonts for use in game and blog.

20/04–26/04: After discussion of possible “shooter” enemy type during team meeting, created sprite for it.

27/04–03/05: Created health bar and a frame for the scoreboard screen.

04/05–10/05: Was focused on other deadlines and no assets were requested.

11/05–17/05: Made a button (which was intended to be animated but aseprite masking was not ideal for what I wanted), a background for the scoreboard which I was initially having issues attaching to an email and then forgot about. Made eponymous Planet Granite using the pixel planet generator.

Iesha's post

During the project, I contributed to the visual design of the game by creating a moodboard to establish the overall aesthetic and creative direction. I then developed pixel art assets in Piskel, including spaceships, planets, and fuel graphics, ensuring they matched the sci-fi style of the game while remaining clear during gameplay. I also used IBM Granite to support idea generation, refine visual concepts, and consider how the assets could improve the player experience.
