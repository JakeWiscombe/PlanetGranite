Reflections & References

Blog Development - Reflection

Alongside the technical debugging and planning of the website, I worked closely with Ildi throughout the development of our blog to ensure that both the visual style and functionality remained consistent. We regularly shared progress, discussed design decisions and reviewed each other’s work to identify problems early and where there is room for improvement. This collaborative workflow helped us maintain a unified aesthetic across the project, while also allowing us to divide responsibilities efficiently. For example, when I was focusing on debugging the home page and ensuring that the rocket’s movement was smooth, Ildi contributed to developing the individual pages ready to fill them with content to document our team’s work and progress.

Game Development - Reflection

During the feedback session, we were encouraged to integrate Granite more deeply into gameplay so it would influence player decisions rather than only acting as a coding assistant on planets. Ideas included requiring Granite to start the spaceship or help plan routes between planets.

Granite was used throughout development to generate early movement and interaction scripts. While the generated code provided a strong starting point, it still required manual adjustments to fit the project structure and gameplay needs. Overall, Granite sped up development, but human input remained essential for refinement and integration.

Granite was also integrated directly into the game as an interactive chatbot. Players can ask questions, receive hints, and access context-aware responses through a custom RAG model based on the game’s lore and mechanics, making Granite part of the user experience rather than only a development tool.

The team divided responsibilities across blog development, Unity and LLM integration, and art and UI design. Regular communication and team calls helped track progress, although more consistent cross-team check-ins and shared testing would have improved collaboration further.

Overall, the project highlighted both the strengths and limitations of Granite as a development assistant, showing its value when combined with human oversight.
