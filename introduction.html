Introduction

Project Overview

The aim of this project was to explore how IBM’s Granite LLM could be used within the development of a space-themed game and accompanying collection of mini-game experiments. The brief encouraged experimentation with Granite both as a development tool and as a potential in-game component, allowing teams to creatively integrate AI into different areas of the project.

Our team chose to focus on using Granite primarily as a development tool to support idea generation, coding support, debugging and problem-solving throughout the design and development process.

The overall concept of our project was heavily inspired by space exploration and progression, which influenced both the game itself and the design of this development blog. Rather than creating a traditional written report, we wanted the blog to feel immersive and interactive while still documenting our full creative journey. To achieve this, we designed the website around a planetary navigation system where users travel through different stages of development using a rocket animation connected by SVG flight paths. This helped reinforce the visual identity of the project while making the documentation process more engaging for creative technology students.

This blog documents the full development journey of the project, including concept development, experimentation, proof-of-concept creation, technical challenges, feedback implementation and final outcomes. It also reflects on how the team adapted to problems encountered throughout development and how collaboration influenced the final result. Overall, the project aimed to demonstrate how AI tools such as Granite can meaningfully support both the creative and technical aspects of game development while encouraging experimentation and innovation within digital design.
